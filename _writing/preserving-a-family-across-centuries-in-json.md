My father spent 36 years in Cameroon's public service—a career measured in decades, not quarters. When I set out to document his biography and our family's genealogical tree, I discovered that the act of encoding lineage into a structured data format is itself an act of preservation.

## The weight of oral history

In the West Region of Cameroon, family history lives in the memories of elders. It is transmitted through conversation, ceremony, and proximity. When that chain is broken—by distance, by death, by the simple passage of time—knowledge disappears without a trace.

## Structure as commitment

Four royal chefferies, seven generations, and a migration from Baleng to Balengou that began in the 1770s—all rendered in a JSON file and an interactive HTML page hosted on GitHub. The choice of format was deliberate. JSON is machine-readable, version-controlled, and portable. It can be parsed by software that does not yet exist.

## A repository does not forget

The medium is the commitment. A Git repository maintains a complete history of every change. It does not expire when a subscription lapses. It persists alongside the code and the ideas it carries. For a family that spans centuries, this durability matters.
